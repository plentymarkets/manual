.Installing the Etsy plugin
====
The interface to Etsy is integrated via a link:https://marketplace.plentymarkets.com/en/plugins/sales/marktplaetze/etsy_4689[plugin^]. Download the Etsy plugin in the plentyMarketplace in order to use it.

[#100]
== Registering with Etsy

In order to set up Etsy in your plentymarkets system, you have to register as a Etsy seller first.

[#200]
== Installing the Etsy plugin in plentymarkets

After buying the Etsy plugin in the plentyMarketplace, install the market in the *Plugins » Plugin overview* menu. Select the filter *Not installed* or *All* to display plugins that have not yet been installed. For further information about downloading and installing plugins, see <<plugins/adding-plugins-system#marketplace-plugins, Adding plugins to the system>>.

[#250]
=== Activating the interface

First, the interface has to be activated in the *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Authentication* menu. This is to grant plentymarkets access to your Etsy account.

[.instruction]
Activating the interface:

. Go to *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Authentication*.
. Click on the globe icon (icon:globe[role="green"]). +
→ A new window opens and you are forwarded to Etsy.
. Enter your Etsy access data.
. Click on *Gestatte den Zugang* (Grant access). +
→ The interface to Etsy is activated and you can carry out further settings.


[#300]
=== Setting up Etsy in plentymarkets

[IMPORTANT]
.Pay attention to the Renewal options at Etsy
====
Prior to setting up Etsy in plentymarkets, you have to set the setting *Renewal options* to *Manual* in the *Shop-Manager » Listings »* _Open listing_ menu at Etsy. Otherwise, the Etsy plugin will not work correctly.
====

Go to *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Settings* to activate the data exchange.

[.instruction]
Activating the data exchange:

. Go to *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Settings*.
. In the *Processes* area, activate the *Item export*, *Stock update*, and *Order import*.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.

[.subhead]
Setting up export languages

In the *Export languages* area, you carry out settings for export languages. The *Shop language* is exported by default. Other languages are used for translations of your offers.

[.instruction]
Setting up export languages:

. Go to *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Settings*.
. Place a check mark next to the languages that your offers should be translated into. Available languages are *English*, *German*, and *French*. +
→ *Note:* The defined *Shop language* is exported by default.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.


[#400]
== Activating the order referrer

In order to link items with Etsy, you have to activate the order referrer Etsy in the *Setup » Orders » Order referrer* menu.

[.instruction]
Activating the order referrer for Etsy:

. Go to *Setup » Orders » Order referrer*.
. Place a check mark next to *Etsy*.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.


[#500]
== Setting the variation availability

Items have to be available for Etsy. This is done in the *Availability* tab of an item within the *Item » Edit item »* _Open item_ *» Tab: Variation ID* menu.

[TIP]
.Selling all variations of an item on Etsy
====
To sell all variations of an item on Etsy, carry out the settings described below for the main variation. The settings are then passed on to all variations of the item.
====

[.instruction]
Setting the variation availability for Etsy:

. Go to *Item » Edit item »* _Open item_ *» Tab: Variation ID » Tab: Settings*.
. Open the variation that you want to sell on Etsy.
. In the *Availability* area, activate the option *Active*.
. Click on the *Availability* tab.
. In the *Markets* area, click in the selection field. +
→ A list of all available markets is displayed.
. Activate the option *Etsy*.
. Click on *Add* (icon:plus-square[role="green"]). +
→ The market is added.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings. +
→ The item is available on Etsy.

If you already sell on Etsy, save Etsy listing IDs as SKUs for the referrer *Etsy* in the *Item » Edit item »* _Open item_ *» Tab: Variation ID » Tab: Settings »Tab: Availability* menu. By doing so, items will be assigned to the items already listed on Etsy during the item export. This is to prevent overselling. +

The Etsy listing ID must be saved in the following format: *Etsy listing ID-plentymarkets Variation ID*, for example *708093072-3467*.


[#600]
== Defining a sales price

Proceed as described below to define a sales price for the order referrer Etsy. This price will be displayed on Etsy.

[.instruction]
Defining a sales price for Etsy:

. Go to *Setup » Item » Sales prices »* _Open sales price_ *» Tab: Settings*.
. Place a check mark next to the referrer *Etsy*.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.


[#700]
== Creating catalogues

Item data is exported to Etsy via catalogue templates. In order to export your items to Etsy, you have to create a catalogue template in the *Data » Catalogs* menu. Link the Etsy data fields with item data saved in plentymarkets in the catalogue template afterwards. The catalogue templates are automatically exported to Etsy once a day if you have activated the *Item export* in the *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Settings* menu. +

Use the *Listing - Etsy* catalogue template for Etsy.

For further information about catalogues, refer to the <<data/exporting-data/managing-catalogues#, Managing catalogues>> page.

[NOTE]
.Visibility of the *Data » Catalogues* menu
====
The menu *Data » Catalogs* only becomes visible after you have installed a plugin which provides a catalogue template.
====

[.instruction]
Creating a catalogue:

. Go to *Data » Catalogues*.
. Click on *Create catalogue*.
. Enter a name for the catalogue.
. Select the *Listing - Etsy* catalogue template from the drop-down list.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings. +
→ The catalogue is created. +
→ The catalogue is opened for further editing.

After you have created a catalogue, link the market's data fields with item data saved in plentymarkets. For further information about the plentymarkets data sources that can be linked, refer to the <<data/exporting-data/managing-catalogues#_mapping_data_sources, Mapping data sources>> chapter.

[.instruction]
Linking data fields in the catalogue:

. Go to *Data » Catalogues*.
. Click on the catalogue that you have created. +
→ The catalogue opens. +
→ The market's data fields are displayed on the left-hand side. +
→ *Tip:* Mandatory fields are marked with an asterisk.
. Select a plentymarkets data source for the market's data fields from the drop-down list.
. Click on *Add source* to assign another plentymarkets data source to a data field. +
→ A new line is displayed. +
→ *Note:* Only one value is exported even if you assign more than one data source to a data field. The data is checked in the order in which you assigned it. This means that if the first data field does not provide a value, the second data field is exported etc.
. Link the desired data fields. +
→ All mandatory fields marked with an * must be linked to a plentymarkets data source.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.

[.subhead]
Specifics of the Etsy catalogue template

The *Shop sections* data field in the *Listing - Etsy* template provides all shop sections that you created at Etsy. The shop sections can be selected from the drop-down list and can be linked to data saved in plentymarkets.


[#800]
== Updating Etsy categories

In order to update Etsy categories in plentymarkets, delete your credentials in the *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Authentication* menu and add them again.


[.instruction]
Updating Etsy categories:

. Go to *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Authentication*.
. Click on *Delete* to delete the credentials.
. Click on the globe icon (icon:globe[role="green"]) to add new credentials. +
→ You are forwarded to Etsy.
. Click on *Allow access* at Etsy. +
→ New credentials are created and saved in plentymarkets. +
→ The Etsy categories are updated.


[#900]
== Transferring legal information

In order to transfer legal information to Etsy, you have to save the information for each language you use for Etsy. The legal information is then added to the item description.

[.instruction]
Transferring legal information:

. Go to *Setup » Markets » Etsy » Legal information*. +
→ The window *Legal information* opens.
. Select the language in which you want to save legal information.
. Enter the text.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.

[#1200]
== Assigning user rights

Users with *Back end* access need rights to use the Etsy plugin. Open the *Setup » Settings » User » Rights » User* menu to assign rights.

[.instruction]
Assigning user rights for back end users:

. Go to *Setup » Settings » User » Rights » User*.
. Use the search function and open the user that should be edited.
. Expand the *Authorisation: Markets* menu.
. Select the setting *Credentials*.
. *Save* (icon:save[role="green"]) the settings.
