[IMPORTANT]
.Notes on the Managing contacts page of the manual
====
This page of the manual describes all settings that are available in the **CRM » Contacts** menu in the new Terra back end. The <<crm/managing-customer-data#, **Managing customer data**>> page of the manual that describes the settings in the **Customers » Edit customers** and **System » Customers** menus will be available as well. When the new **CRM » Contacts** menu replaces the old **Customers » Edit customers** menu completely, the **Customers » Edit customers** menu will disappear and associated with that the **Managing customer data** page of the manual will be removed.
====

The **CRM » Contacts** menu allows you to create data records for your contacts and to save all relevant information associated with the contacts. You can also edit and delete your contacts in this menu and display the addresses of your contacts. Furthermore, it is possible to display the history. The history lists e.g. when the contact registered in your online store or whether he or she has a regular account or a guest account.

[#100]
== Creating a contact

Proceed as described below to create a new contact. Add contact options such as email addresses, telephone numbers and, if required, the VAT number. You can also create a new contact, for example, while taking an order over the phone or while filling out a written order. It is also possible to save the contact details of interested parties here. When creating a contact, the system will automatically assign an ID. This ID cannot be changed.

[.instruction]
Creating a contact:

. Go to **CRM » Contacts**.
. Click on **Create new contact**.
. Carry out the settings in the **New contact** section. Pay attention to the information given in table 1.
. Open the **Contact options** section and click on **Add contact option**.
. Select the type and the sub-type and enter the value. Pay attention to the information given in table 1.
. **Save** the settings.

__**Important**__: The sales representative selected in the data record of the contact will not be taken into account for the orders. == Searching for a contact

In the filter section on the left side, you can search for contacts according to specific criteria. The contacts found will be listed in the overview in the **Results** section. You can edit the contact data.

In order to search for specific contacts, enter a search term in the search fields or select the appropriate drop-down menu settings. You can enter multiple search terms into the search fields at one time. This makes your search run quicker and be more accurate.

[IMPORTANT]
.Searching contacts by means of the email address
====
Please note that it is currently possible to search contacts by means of the email address only. The other search options will be gradually added.
====

[.instruction]
Searching for a contact:

. Go to **CRM » Contacts**.
. In the filter section, enter the search term in the search field or set the filter options in the drop-down menus. Pay attention to the information given in table 2.
. Click on **Search**. +
→ The contacts that correspond to the search criteria entered will be listed.

.Filters for contacts
[cols="a,a"]
|====
|Setting |Explanation

|**Search**
|Click on **Search** without selecting any filter settings. This will display all contacts. Alternatively, limit the search results by using filters and click on **Search**. The contacts found will be listed in the overview.

|**Reset**
|Resets the filter criteria that was entered.

|**ID**
|Each contact automatically receives a consecutive and unique ID. It doesn't matter if the data record was saved manually or automatically. Two contacts never have the same ID. The ID is always a real number. "00521" or "ADF8354" e.g. are invalid.

|**Surname**
|Enter the first name and/or surname to search for the contact with this name.

|**Email**
|Enter the email address to search for contacts with this email address.

|**Address**
|Enter the name of the street, the postcode or the town that is saved for the contact.

|**Country**
|Select a country to only display contacts from this country. The country is saved within the contact data.

|**Client (store)**
|Select a client (store) to only display contacts that correspond to this client. The client (store) is saved within the contact data. +

== Editing a contact

Use the <<crm/_managing-contacts#200, **filter settings**>> to search for the contact(s). Open the contact by clicking in the line of the contact to edit the contact's data such as the language, class, date of birth, telephone number and email address, for example. You will find explanations of the settings and options in the sections **Contact** and **Contact options** in the <<crm/_managing-contacts#100, **Creating a contact**>> chapter.

[.instruction]
Editing a contact:

. Go to **CRM » Contacts**.
. In the filter section, enter the search term in the search field or set the filter options in the drop-down menus. Pay attention to the information given in table 2 in the <<crm/_managing-contacts#200, **Searching for a contact**>> chapter.
. Click on **Search**.
. Click in the line of the contact that you want to edit. +
→ The contact will open.
. Carry out the changes as desired.
. **Save** the settings.

== Deleting a contact

You can delete individual contacts in the **CRM » Contacts** menu. All addresses that are assigned to the contact will be deleted if they are not linked to an order.

[.instruction]
Deleting a contact:

. Go to **CRM » Contacts**.
. Search the contact with the help of <<crm/_managing-contacts#200, **filter settings**>>. +
→ The contacts that correspond to the search criteria entered will be displayed.
. Then click on the contact in the overview to open and edit the data record.
. Click on **Delete contact** on the left side. +
→ A window will open and you need to confirm your decision.
. Click on **OK**. +
→ The contact will be deleted and removed from the overview.

[#600]
== Displaying addresses

When the contact is open, you can view all addresses that are assigned to the contact at a glance. The addresses are displayed in info boxes. The tag on the info box tells you if the address is the invoice address of the delivery address.

[.instruction]
Displaying addresses:

. Go to **CRM » Contacts**.
. Search the contact with the help of <<crm/_managing-contacts#200, **filter settings**>>. +
→ The contacts that correspond to the search criteria entered will be displayed.
. Then click on the contact in the overview to open and edit the data record.
. Click on **Addresses** on the left side. +
→ All addresses that are assigned to the contact will be displayed.

== Displaying the history

Each data record contains the history of a contact. The history includes e.g. how long the contact has been registered and when he or she has been logged into your online store for the last time and when the last order was generated. This data will be automatically updated and cannot be changed.

[.instruction]
Displaying the history:

. Go to **CRM » Contacts**.
. In the filter section, enter the search terms in the search fields or set the filter options in the drop-down menus. Pay attention to the information given in table 2 in the <<crm/_managing-contacts#200, **Searching for a contact**>> chapter.
. Click on **Search**.
. Click in the line of the contact that you want to edit. +
→ The contact will open and the history will be displayed.
. Pay attention to the information about the history given in table 3.

.History in the **CRM » Contacts » Open contact** menu
[cols="a,a"]
|====
|Setting |Explanation

|**Registered since**
|Date and time when the contact has first registered.

|**Updated**
|Date and time when the data record of the contact has been updated for the last time.

|**Last login**
|Date and time when the contact was logged into your online store for the last time.

|**Last order**
|Date and time when the last order was made. The order ID will also be displayed. Click on the ID to open the order. If no order has been placed, **No data** is displayed in this field.

|**Account type**
|Account via which the contact has registered. **Regular account** means that the customer has newly registered in your online store. **Guest account** means that the customer has ordered as a guest without registering in your online store.
|====
